PUSH

Copyright (c) 2001 Karl Scherer

This game is played on a 8x8 square board, with the following setup:
MOVES - By moving the last stone in a row the player can push one or more stones as far as empty spaces are available, but not more than 3 cells. Push moves are either orthogonally or diagonally.
Forward moves have priority over sideways or backward.
There are no captures in this game.
GOAL - A player who fills the opposite two rows wins.

An example

Black's turn. The game is ending. The player ahead in the final positioning race will win the game. Black move c6-c7, then e4-f3, d5-d6, f3-f2-f1, d6-c7-b8, a3-a2, d7-d8, a2-a1, e6-d7 and Black wins.
