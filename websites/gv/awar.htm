Abstract WAR

This game is played on a 10x10 Hex board, with some special cells called domination cells (DCs), like in the next image.
  • GOAL - To control more than 50% of the board's DCs.
  • PLAYERS - 2 to 6 players.
  • SETUP - Each player has 4 soldiers placed in the DCs of a corner. For 5 players, the 4 DCs in the remaining empty corner are eliminated. For 2, 3, 4 and 6 players, there is always balanced ways to occupy corners.
  • TURN - Each player presents his orders. All Help orders are valid until the player moves again. So, a player may help other player's soldiers. A variant of this rule, is that all players move at the same time.
  • STRENGTH - Every order has initial strength of 1.
  • SOLDIER - A soldier may receive 3 kinds of orders:
    • HOLD - hold its position.
    • MOVE - moves to a specified legal cell. Each soldier can move through 3 empty hexagons.
    • HELP - helps another soldier's order. Each Help order increases the strength by 1. A help is legal if that soldier could move to the place where the helped soldier is. If the helping piece is attacked, then the help is transformed into an Hold order (except if the help is against the soldier that is attacking, see example 3). 
    • GUARD - A soldier that orders guard, will defend its adjacent empty cells with force 1. This means that another soldier can only pass in one of those cells with superior strength. A guard order can be increased by an help order. A cell guarded by two soldiers, can only be crossed with strength 3.
  • CONFLICT - If two orders interfere, wins that with greater strength. If both have equal strengths they are not made (except if one is an help order against the other, then the help order is not canceled). A soldier may enter in several conflicts, in that case, the conflicts are treated sequentially (from the 1st player to the last). All orders in the last sequence of moves (i.e. until the current player moved in the last turn) are valid. The following cases are possible:
    • MOVE > (HOLD or HELP) - if a soldier tries to move into a cell with another soldier, this soldier must retreat to the cell in the opponent direction of the move (mirror symmetry). If the retreat is not possible, the soldier is eliminated.
    • MOVE > MOVE - a soldier tries to move where another soldier has just moved. The soldier with less strength does not move (in parallel turns, this soldier does not move in the first place). If it is the first soldier, then he must retreat, if it is the last soldier, then he stays in the original cell.
  • ADJUST - At the end of each turn, each player must have as many soldiers as DCs. If the number of DCs is greater, he can drop more soldiers in his unoccupied DCs (if he does not have unoccupied DCs, he must wait). If he has more soldiers, he must remove as many soldiers as needed in order to get the same number of DCs.
  • AGREEMENT - [optional rule] An agreement is a compromise (in game theory, a compromise is a move that reduces the future move range of one or more players) that some players may agree to follow. The syntax is ACCEPT( type, players, time, other-players ), where players are the set of players will comply and send this order (if any players does not send the order, the agreement is void), time is for how long the agreement must take place (the maximum time is 10 turns) and type may be:
    • peace - the players will not move or help a move against their soldiers (however, they can help another help of an hostile move).
    • neutral - the players may not help any move from other-players set.
    • war - the players are oblige to move against or help any soldier that can move against an other-player's soldier (if there are several choices, the player can choose from one of them).
    • Example: ACCEPT( war, red+black, 7, green )

    • Let's check some examples (all samples use the same image below):
    Example 1:   Retreat
    • Blue C3 HOLD | B5 HELP C3
    • Red A6 MOVE B5 | D9 HELP A6
    • Result: The Blue soldier B5 must retreat to the symmetric cell of the attacker direction, i.e., cell C4.
    Example 2: Retreat
    • Blue B5 HOLD | C3 HELP B5 | D5 HELP B5
    • Red A6 MOVE B5 | D9 HELP A6 | B7 HELP A6
    • Green F6 MOVE D5
    • Result: B5 had help of two soldiers, but F6 neutralized D5 help, so B5 must retreat again to C4.
    Example 3: 1 is not equal to 2
    • Blue D5 MOVE F6 | C3 HELP D5
    • Green F6 MOVE C3
    • Result: The F6 move would neutralize C3 help, but C3 is helping an attack against F6, so F6 don't neutralize C3, and then F6 must retreat due to the strength 2 attack, to cell H7 (symmetric cell relative to D5).
    Example 4: Same cell move
    • Blue C3 MOVE E4
    • Green F6 MOVE E4
    • Result: With parallel moves, both moves are not made, since they try to move to the same cell with equal force. In sequence moves, the player that moves first goes to E4.
    Example 6: Cell Defense
    • Blue C3 MOVE D4 | D5 MOVE D4
    • Green F6 MOVE C4
    • Result: The Blue soldiers defended cell C4 with force 1 without occupying it (both moves has strength 1). That way, the green soldier was unable to move to C4 (however, if F7 helps F6, then F6's move would succeed, since the total green strength would be 2).
    Example 7: Chain Helps
    Blue D5 MOVE F6 | C3 HELP D5
    Red H10 MOVE I8
    Green H6 HELP I8 | I8 HELP F6
    Result: All green soldiers stay at their positions. The Blue attack cannot succeed (strength 2 vs 2). The Red attack didn't neutralize I8 help because its force is 2 (it had H6 help). If H6 order was neutralized by some reason, then I8 order would be transformed to Hold, and soldier F6 would retreat to H7.
