BABUSCHKA

Copyright (c) 1982 Al Newman

This game is played on the following board:


  • TURN -  On each turn, each player moves a friendly stone (or passes if there are no valid moves).
    • A stone may move forward or sideways to an (orthogonally or diagonally) adjacent cell which must be empty or occupied by a darker (friendly or enemy) stone.
      • Pieces move alone, leaving any stone below at the initial cell.
    • A stone may also jump over an (orthogonally or diagonally) adjacent stone to the immediate next cell which must be empty or occupied by a darker (friendly or enemy) stone.
      • Jumps are multiple (it's not mandatory to maximize jumps) and are only sideways or forward (not backwards). Any piece can jump over any other  piece.
    • Stones under other stones cannot move or jump.
  • GOAL - The winner is whoever moves all of his stones to the last two rows and position them like in the initial setup.

A stone can freeze darker enemy stones by moving over them. Since stones cannot move or jump backwards, if a white or grey cell moves alone into the last row, the player is unable to end the game.

I wish to thank Jerry Khun for pointing me additional rule information (in the original game, the stones are dolls of different sizes, so that dolls can hide smaller dolls. The game may need some memory skills if it is forbidden to note down which dolls are hiding).
